The Whole World

download poster
dir. by Julián Quintanilla, Spain
runtime: 30 min
screening: September 4th
As he does every year, Julián visits his dead mother in the village cemetery. She appears to him and they make plans... more or less 'together': she devises them and he obeys. But this year, Julián is going to get a very special request from his mother that will surprise... the whole world.

How do you bring a mother back from the death?
by Julián Quintanilla

The Whole World’ came about from the need, if only for a short while, to speak again to someone who has gone. In my case, in order to achieve this, there was only one way: by making a film.

After the death of my mother in 2001, at the age of 49, I felt the need to keep on communicating with her in some way. And for me film became the medium, not only to do this, but to provide continuity to a story that destiny wanted to be over and done with, but which for me, is not.

That’s why, every year, I go to see my mother at the cemetery.
And yes, I do talk to her.

Just as my neighbor Felipa did with her dead lady friends and, as it happens, my grandfather did now and then in order to speak to my grandmother.

For some this might signify madness. For others, however, it is perfectly normal. And in the Badajoz neighborhood of San Roque, where I was born, some of us hold the belief that the dead…
are not entirely dead.
